Date: June 10, 2025

The Federal Government of Nigeria has officially approved a new national curriculum for secondary schools, introducing key subjects like digital literacy, entrepreneurship, and financial education as core requirements.

The announcement was made by the Minister of Education, Prof. Tahir Mamman, during a press briefing in Abuja. The curriculum will take effect from the 2025/2026 academic session and is part of efforts to reform the education system and better prepare Nigerian students for the 21st-century workforce.

According to the Ministry, teachers will receive new training modules starting July, and schools will be equipped with digital learning resources, especially in underserved communities.

The government believes the new curriculum will bridge the skills gap, reduce youth unemployment, and boost innovation across sectors. Students will also engage in hands-on community projects and be encouraged to start early ventures before graduation.
