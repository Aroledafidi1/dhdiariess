🌅 The Bread Seller’s Son – A Story of Hope from Ajegunle

Date: June 28, 2025

In the heart of Ajegunle, Lagos, where the streets are alive with the scent of grilled suya and the hum of danfo buses, 14-year-old Michael Ojo starts his morning selling bread before school.

His mother, Mama Ojo, is a familiar face in the area — balancing a tray of agege bread on her head with grace and strength. Despite life’s hardship, she has kept her son in school with nothing but determination and faith.

"We don't have much, but we believe in hard work," she says. Michael agrees — he’s already topping his class in Mathematics at Ajegunle Community High School and dreams of becoming an engineer.

💡 A Spark of Hope

Last month, a short video of Michael studying under a streetlight went viral on social media. Touched by his story, a local NGO awarded him a full scholarship through university, and a tech startup donated a laptop and solar-powered reading lamp.

“I didn’t expect this,” Michael said shyly. “I just want to make my mummy proud.”

🌍 Bigger Than One Family

Michael’s story isn’t just his — it reflects the everyday resilience of many Nigerian families. DHDiaries celebrates the stories of regular people who show us that greatness often begins in quiet places.
