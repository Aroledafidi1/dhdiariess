🇳🇬 ₦200 Billion Youth Development Fund Launched

Date: June 18, 2025

In a bold policy move aimed at tackling unemployment and supporting innovation, President Bola Ahmed Tinubu has unveiled a ₦200 billion Youth Development Fund targeting young Nigerians in the creative, tech, and agricultural sectors.

The fund, which will be managed through the Ministry of Youth Development and the Bank of Industry (BOI), is designed to offer grants and low-interest loans to startup founders, digital creators, music entrepreneurs, agropreneurs, and other youth-led ventures.

🚀 Who Can Benefit?

  • Young Nigerians aged 18–35 with viable business ideas
  • Registered startups and informal entrepreneurs
  • Music and film producers, fashion designers, app developers
  • Youth cooperatives in agriculture and renewable energy

The initiative is part of the administration's wider “Renewed Hope Youth Agenda” and follows increasing calls from youth groups and activists for practical economic reforms.

🗣️ Public Reactions

Youth leaders and advocacy groups have praised the move, urging the government to ensure transparency and regional balance. Critics, however, warned about potential corruption and political favoritism.

“This is the kind of investment that can ignite real change, if done right,” said Omoyele Dada, founder of the Nigerian Innovation Network.
